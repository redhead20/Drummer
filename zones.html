Snare Drum Zones

Snare drums have different zones on the head that create different sounds. Learning the zones helps you understand technique, sound quality, and where each type of note is played.

Center Zone

The center of the drum produces the fullest and strongest sound. Most normal strokes, accents, and diddles are played here unless a notation symbol tells you otherwise.

Center zone

Mid Zone

The mid zone sits between the center and edge. Itâ€™s used for more articulate notes and for hybrid strokes. It has a lighter, slightly higher-pitched sound.

Gut Edge Zone

The gut edge produces the brightest and highest-pitched sound. Techniques like pings or certain visual strokes are played near the edge of the drum.
