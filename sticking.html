Sticking & Notation

Sticking tells you which hand plays each note. In drumline music, sticking is often written as “R” and “L” above or below the notes.

Sticking notation image goes here

R & L Basics

“R” stands for right hand and “L” stands for left hand. Even simple patterns often include specific sticking instructions to make them feel balanced.

  • RLRL = alternating hands
  • RRLL = double strokes

Common Patterns

Many drumline parts use repeated stickings, like paradiddles or double stroke rolls. Learning these makes reading music much easier.

  • Paradiddle: R L R R | L R L L
  • Double Stroke: R R L L R R L L

Connecting to Accents

Sticking and accents work together. Choosing which hand hits each accented note helps you play stronger, cleaner phrases.

  • Plan sticking around accents
  • Keep soft notes controlled
