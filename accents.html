Accents

Accents show which notes should pop out of the pattern. They don’t change the rhythm, but they change the sound and energy of the line.

Accented rhythm image goes here

Basic Accent (>)

A basic accent is shown with a small “>” above the note. Hit these notes slightly louder than the regular notes around them.

  • Placed above the note head
  • Makes the note stand out

Unaccented Notes

Notes without an accent mark should be played softer and more relaxed. The contrast between accented and unaccented notes creates groove.

  • Use a lower stick height
  • Stay relaxed and controlled

Building Accent Patterns

Accents are often placed on specific beats or parts of a phrase to shape the rhythm. You might see them on every “&” or just on downbeats.

  • Helps show musical phrasing
  • Makes patterns more interesting
