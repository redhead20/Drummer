Note Heads

Drumline note heads don’t just show different sounds — they show different techniques. Each note head represents a specific stroke type, visual, or method of contact on the drum.

Snare Note Heads

Each of these symbols represents a technique you’ll see in drumline music.

Hit

Standard stroke played in the center of the drum.

Double Stop

Both hands strike at the same time — used for unisons.

Rim Shot

Head + rim hit together for a powerful, crack-like sound.

Ping

Light stroke near the edge for a bright, articulate sound.

Stick Shot

One stick strikes the other while it rests on the head.

Rim Knock

Tip hits the rim while the butt rests on the head.

Rim

Rim-only stroke used for cross-stick effects.

Backstick / Visual

Struck with the butt end — both a sound and a visual technique.
